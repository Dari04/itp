Day 1: Sunday, October 01, 2017 - Lima

Nestled comfortably on the Pacific Ocean side of South America, Lima offers numerous sights and surprises in this burgeoning city of 8 million inhabitants. In addition to being the capital and the largest city in Peru, Lima is the cultural and economic hub of the country. The city is able to blend this burgeoning metropolitan reputation with its much valued ancient heritage.

Founded by Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro on January 18, 1535, Lima would go on to become the most important city in the Spanish Viceroyalty of Peru, an area that encompassed most of Spain's possessions in South America during the colonial area of the mid-1500s to the early 1800s. During this time Lima grew prosperous and became known as one of the greatest cities in South America. Today that trend continues. Nearly one-third of the nation's population lives in Lima's metropolitan area and the city attracts travelers from many different countries not only as a point of departure but as a destination. While the city seems to be in constant expansion, a peaceful co-existence has been reached between the old ways and the new.

A mix of the old world and the new await each visitor to Peru's capital city. From the abundant number of cultural events and historical sites, to the packed white sand beaches and the breathtaking views from the city's numerous skyscrapers, Peru is a city sure to astound any visitor.

Day 2: Monday, October 02, 2017 – Lima to Cusco

 With many beautiful examples of pre-Colombian architecture, as well as a number of extravagant colonial constructions, Cusco is generally considered to be one of South America's major tourist highlights. Its proximity to Machu Picchu also increases its popularity as a tourist destination. Cusco, in Peru's native Quechua, translates as The Navel of the World; and with its central and elevated position of over 3,500 meters, the ancient Inca capital straddles the Andes with a view over the surrounding countryside.

The center of the Inca universe, Cusco served as hub for a vast network of roads interconnecting virtually the whole of South America. Traditional Inca society had a complex social and political organization, as well as advanced architectural engineering, and medicinal and agricultural innovations, many examples of which can be seen during a tour of the city. In 1534, Francisco Pizarro arrived with the Conquistadores and after conquering the mighty empire, the Spanish began to plunder and then rebuild the ancient city in accordance with their vision.

Today Cusco has firmly established itself as a tourist Mecca, and travelers from all over the world make their way to the old Inca capital to take in the city's awesome sites. The many ruins both in and around the city are of course magnificent, and coupled with a trip to both the fertile hills of the Sacred Valley, and of course the Lost Citadel of Machu Picchu, a trip to Cusco will be truly unforgettable. With so much to see and do, Cusco is a must see for anyone planning on visiting Peru.

Day 1 of the Inka Trail: Inca Trail to Machu Picchu (Wiñaywayna-Aqua Calientes)

You will be picked up from your hotel (Please take into account that the pick up time will be reconfirmed during your briefing) to be transferred to either Poroy train station or Ollantaytambo train station; from this point you will trip until de starting point of your trail. The trail starts with crossing the Willkamayu River by a bridge; also known as the Urubamba River. After you have crossed the river you will be visiting the famous Inca site of Chachabamba. After an introduction of the Inca history you will start walking a trail of roughly 8 km. After 3 hours of walking you will reach the archaeological site of Wiñaywayna (2650 m). Wiñaywayna is also known as the second most impressive archaeological site after Machu Picchu. This fantastic place is a perfect example of Inca terrace farming, and it was probably an experimental laboratory for agricultural purposes in Inca times. The name Wiñaywayna means ´forever young´. After walking around this beautiful place you will continue your trek by crossing the rain forest, through a narrow path, until you reach Inti Punku (Sun Gate). At this point you will have a great view over the most impressive place: Machu Picchu. After a break we will continue by bus to Aguas Calientes where you will spend the night. This evening you will have the chance to visit and relax in the hot springs that are located at the upper section of Aguas Calientes.

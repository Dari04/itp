Your private guide will pick you up for your first day exploring the Sacred Valley where you'll be able to stop at hidden viewpoints along the way and travel through the region at your own pace. You'll stop in Pisac and visit its famed artisans' and antiques market which is considered to be one of Peru's liveliest. After exploring Pisac's market you'll visit the Inca ruins above the town of Pisac which offer spectacular views over the Urubamba River, agricultural terraces and intricate Inca waterworks. Continuing your journey through the Sacred Valley you'll reach the exclusive Hacienda Huayoccari where you'll be treated to a gourmet lunch. This 17th Century estate and private museum is owned by the Sacred Valley's well-known Lambarri Orihuela family. The restaurant's secluded location offers expansive views over the Sacred Valley backed by the Andes Mountains and the opulent manor has an extensive private collection of art.

Day 2 Journey to Medellin

Description

Cusco, located at over 11,000ft. above sea level, means that acclimatizing to the altitude is essential to enjoying your time in the Peruvian Andes. For that reason, upon arrival at the Cusco Airport, your private guide and vehicle will take you down some 3,150ft. into the scenic Sacred Valley with your first stop being in the small Andean village of Chincheros. At the Chincheros Weaver's Market you'll learn about the traditional dying techniques used and watch as the locals work the looms to produce beautiful, bright and colorful blankets and garments. We help support the women's cooperative and local community by simply visiting the Chincheros Weaver's Market where you can find and purchase beautifully handwoven alpaca and vicuña clothing.
